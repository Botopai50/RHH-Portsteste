# Work With Me

I make software run where it isn't meant to. Most of my public work is porting PC and indie games to Linux ARM handhelds. They use the same kind of processors that you find in your mobile phone or your Amazon Firestick. Weak hardware, OpenGLES, tight memory, no vendor support, you name it. Optimization and tradeoff is the game.

My ports and guides are free, and they stay free. This page is about something separate: private, dedicated work for commercial projects. If you're a studio or publisher who needs your own game handled on your schedule and to your spec, I'm available for contract hire.

Plenty of capable people in this scene will tell you, for free, whether a game can be ported. What I do is the part after that: work out *why* it doesn't run, figure out the path to making it, and then actually do it. The diagnosis is the easy half and it's everywhere. The detailed work of making something feasible and then making it real is the challenge I'm here for.

I take on select freelance and contract work. I prefer scoped, asynchronous projects over open-ended commitments or on-call arrangements.

## What I do

- **Cross-platform porting.** Getting games and software running natively on Linux, Steam Deck, ARM, and Android, including the wrapper, translation, and runtime work that involves (GameMaker, Godot, Wine/box64, GLES).
- **QA and platform testing.** Structured, reproducible bug reports across builds, regression testing, and catching the crashes and performance problems that only surface on real low-end hardware — before they reach players or storefront review.
- **Build and release engineering.** CI, cross-compilation, runtime bundling, and installers.
- **Getting stalled things moving.** Legacy or decompiled codebases that won't build on modern toolchains, or are stuck partway. I work out what's broken and get it running again, and I write down what I did.
